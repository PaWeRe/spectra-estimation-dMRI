\section{Conclusion}

Bayesian spectral decomposition of extended b-value prostate DWI shows that tumor-detection signal collapses onto two coupled outer compartments---restricted intracellular water rising and free luminal water falling as cellularity increases---which a single ADC value already captures, explaining why ADC is so hard to improve upon for detection. The contribution of the full spectrum is not a better detector but a mechanistic account of which compartments drive the contrast, together with the finding that only those two compartments are reliably identifiable from a clinical acquisition while the grade-relevant intermediate compartments are not. MAP provides fast, stable point estimates sufficient for classification; the Bayesian posterior adds, at no extra cost, per-component identifiability and a propagated per-prediction uncertainty that point estimates cannot. Resolving the grading axis that lives in the less-identifiable intermediate compartments is the natural next target for larger, histology-correlated studies.
