\begin{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Purpose:}
Diffusion-weighted imaging (DWI), summarized by the apparent diffusion coefficient (ADC), anchors prostate cancer diagnosis, yet which compartments it resolves, and why one scalar works so well, remain unclear. Bayesian spectral decomposition of extended b-value prostate DWI was used to quantify which compartments are recoverable and explain ADC's near-optimality for region-of-interest (ROI) tumor detection.

\noindent\textbf{Theory and Methods:}
Multi-b DWI (15 b-values to 3500~s/mm$^2$) from 56 patients (149 ROIs: 40 tumor, 109 normal) was decomposed into eight diffusivity components by constrained-ridge maximum a posteriori (MAP) and fully Bayesian No-U-Turn Sampler (NUTS) inference, the latter also inferring per-ROI noise and component uncertainty. Zone-specific leave-one-out logistic regression compared ADC with spectral feature sets (patient-level bootstrap confidence intervals, CIs).

\noindent\textbf{Results:}
Restricted diffusion and free water were precisely estimated (posterior coefficient of variation $0.20$ and $0.32$); intermediate fractions were small and weakly identified, a data limit not a prior artifact. Tumor--normal variation collapsed onto one restricted-to-free-water axis: the eight-fraction classifier matched ADC (area under the ROC curve $0.93$/$0.92$ vs.\ $0.95$/$0.98$, peripheral/transition; overlapping CIs), these two alone matched it ($0.93$/$0.94$), and ADC was near-perfectly anti-correlated with the optimal spectral discriminant ($|r| > 0.97$). Tentative grade-dependent shifts occupied these bins, where ADC is least sensitive---restricted rose, glandular fell with grade. Propagating the posterior added no detection value.

\noindent\textbf{Conclusion:}
For detection the diffusivity spectrum is diagnostically equivalent to ADC, which it mechanistically explains rather than supersedes: detection rides one restricted-to-free-water axis. The Bayesian treatment quantifies what the acquisition resolves; grade-dependent shifts and exploratory pixel-wise maps mark where the spectrum may extend beyond it.

\end{abstract}
