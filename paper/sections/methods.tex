\section{Methods}

\subsection{Patient Cohort and MRI Acquisition}

Retrospective multi-b DWI data were obtained from the cohort previously described by \citet{langkilde2018evaluation}, acquired under an institutional review board--approved protocol; all analyses used de-identified region-of-interest data.
For the 56 included patients, age ranged from 43 to 77 years (mean 62 years) and the mean serum prostate-specific antigen (PSA) level was 8.8~ng/mL (range, 0.37--46~ng/mL).
The cohort included patients with biopsy-confirmed cancer, 7 with benign biopsies, and 1 with high-grade prostatic intraepithelial neoplasia; these latter eight contribute only normal-tissue ROIs and serve as a specificity check against false-positive labeling of non-malignant tissue (@patrick: what does this mean, did we actually do some kind of test? All patients underwent a standard multiparametric MR examination with an additional diffusion-weighted sequence on a GE 3~T Discovery MR750 scanner (GE Medical Systems, Milwaukee, WI).
An endorectal coil (Medrad, Pittsburgh, PA) was used in combination with pelvic phased-array coils for signal reception, providing high signal-to-noise ratio at the cost of a non-routine setup.
The added DWI sequence used a matrix size of $64 \times 64$, field of view $280 \times 280\;\text{mm}^2$, slice thickness 5~mm without gap, TR~4000~ms, and TE~100~ms.
The relatively long echo time was required to accommodate the high diffusion weighting with gradient times $\delta = 37$~ms and $\Delta = 45$~ms.
Fifteen b-values were acquired in 250~s/mm$^2$ increments from $b = 0$ to $3500\;\text{s/mm}^2$ along each of three orthogonal diffusion encoding directions; total scan time for the extended-range acquisition was 3~min.
For the ROI-level analysis, the per-direction signal intensities were combined into a trace decay (geometric mean of the three orthogonal directions per b-value).

A total of 149 regions of interest (ROIs) were annotated: 40 tumor and 109 normal tissue, spanning peripheral zone (PZ; 81 ROIs: 27 tumor, 54 normal) and transition zone (TZ; 68 ROIs: 13 tumor, 55 normal).
Gleason Grade Group (GGG) labels from biopsy were available for 29 tumor ROIs (20 low-grade GGG~1--2, 9 high-grade GGG~3--5).

\subsection{ADC Computation}

Apparent diffusion coefficients were computed by monoexponential least-squares fitting of $\log S(b) = \log S_0 - b \cdot \text{ADC}$ to the raw signal at b-values $\leq 1000\;\text{s/mm}^2$ (five measurements per ROI: $b = 0, 250, 500, 750, 1000\;\text{s/mm}^2$) (@patrick: can we be sure that this is how ADC values for all figs and table 1 were computed, please double check python code and explain method to me again for my undersatnding).
This follows PI-RADS~v2.1, which specifies a maximum b-value of 800--1000~s/mm$^2$ for ADC maps \citep{turkbey2019prostate, weinreb2016pirads}; the b-value range materially affects the absolute ADC value \citep{maier2022choice}.

\subsection{Classification}

Tumor detection was evaluated by zone with $L_2$-regularized logistic regression under leave-one-out cross-validation (LOOCV): tumor versus normal in the peripheral zone ($n = 81$) and the transition zone ($n = 68$), fit separately because normal peripheral- and transition-zone tissue differ systematically in baseline diffusion whereas tumor tissue is comparatively similar across zones, so a pooled boundary would conflate zonal and pathological variation.
Four feature representations were scored through one common pipeline---ADC alone, each individual spectral fraction alone, the two outer fractions $\{D = 0.25,\,3.0\;\umsmm\}$ jointly, and all eight fractions (MAP or NUTS posterior means)---so that single- and multi-feature models are directly comparable.
Within each fold, features were standardized and the classifier ($C = 1.0$) was fit on the training ROIs and applied to the held-out ROI, with standardization and fitting confined to the training fold to prevent leakage; the conventional in-sample ADC raw-rank AUC is additionally reported as a reference baseline (Table~\ref{tab:auc}).
Performance was the area under the receiver-operating-characteristic curve (AUC), with $95\%$ confidence intervals from patient-level percentile bootstrap (2000 resamples) \citep{efron1993bootstrap}; sensitivity analyses at $C = 0.1$ and $C = 10$ confirmed robustness to regularization strength.
