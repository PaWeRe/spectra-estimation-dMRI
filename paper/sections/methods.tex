\section{Methods}

\subsection{Patient Cohort and MRI Acquisition}

Retrospective multi-b DWI data were obtained from the cohort previously described by \citet{langkilde2018evaluation}; their retrospective use was approved by the institutional review board. \stephan{please confirm the IRB/consent wording for the BWH cohort.}
For the 56 included patients, age ranged from 43 to 77 years (mean 62) and mean serum prostate-specific antigen was 8.8~ng/mL (range 0.37--46).
The cohort comprised patients with biopsy-confirmed cancer together with 7 with benign biopsies and 1 with high-grade prostatic intraepithelial neoplasia; these eight contribute only normal-tissue ROIs.

All patients underwent a standard multiparametric examination with an additional diffusion-weighted sequence on a GE 3~T Discovery MR750 (GE Medical Systems, Milwaukee, WI). An endorectal coil (Medrad, Pittsburgh, PA) combined with pelvic phased-array coils provided high signal-to-noise ratio at the cost of a non-routine setup. The DWI sequence used a $64 \times 64$ matrix, field of view $280 \times 280\;\text{mm}^2$, 5~mm slices without gap, TR~4000~ms, and TE~100~ms (the long echo time accommodating the high diffusion weighting with $\delta = 37$~ms and $\Delta = 45$~ms). Fifteen b-values in 250~s/mm$^2$ increments from $0$ to $3500\;\text{s/mm}^2$ were acquired along three orthogonal directions (total scan time 3~min); per-direction intensities were combined into a trace decay (geometric mean) for the ROI analysis.

A total of 149 ROIs were annotated: 40 tumor and 109 normal, spanning peripheral zone (PZ; 81 ROIs: 27 tumor, 54 normal) and transition zone (TZ; 68 ROIs: 13 tumor, 55 normal). Gleason Grade Group (GGG) labels from biopsy were available for 29 tumor ROIs (20 low-grade GGG~1--2, 9 high-grade GGG~3--5).

\subsection{Spectral Estimation}

For each ROI, the trace decay was decomposed into eight diffusivity components ($D \in \{0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 3.0, 20.0\}\;\umsmm$) by both MAP and Bayesian estimation as described in Theory. MAP spectra (Eq.~\ref{eq:map}) used ridge strength $\lambda = 10^{-3}$, tuned to optimize fraction-of-mass recovery on simulated spectra (Figure~\ref{fig:validation}); computation was instantaneous ($< 1\;\text{s}$ for all 149 ROIs). \note{Patrick: optional Supporting figure---a $\lambda$ sweep (mass recovery vs $\lambda$) to show the tuning had rigor and to note the over-smoothing at the historic reference $\lambda = 0.1$.} The Bayesian posterior (Eqs.~\ref{eq:prior_R}--\ref{eq:likelihood}) was sampled per ROI with NUTS via PyMC, using 2000 post-tuning draws, 200 tuning steps, 4 chains, and target acceptance 0.95; all 149 ROIs converged (split-$\hat{R} < 1.05$ \citep{vehtari2021rank}). Posterior means and MAP estimates were rescaled to sum to one for a fractional representation; this post-hoc rescaling preserves the relative spectrum and does not alter the discriminant, which standardizes features. Per-component identifiability is summarized by the posterior coefficient of variation (CV; defined in Theory), reported as both the cohort-averaged CV and the absolute posterior standard deviation.

\subsection{ADC and Classification}

ADC was computed by monoexponential least-squares fitting of $\log S(b) = \log S_0 - b \cdot \text{ADC}$ to the raw signal at b-values $\leq 1000\;\text{s/mm}^2$ (five measurements: $b = 0, 250, 500, 750, 1000$), following the PI-RADS specification of a maximum b-value of 800--1000~s/mm$^2$ \citep{turkbey2019prostate, weinreb2016pirads}; the b-value range materially affects the absolute ADC \citep{maier2022choice}.

Tumor detection was evaluated by $L_2$-regularized logistic regression with leave-one-out cross-validation (LOOCV) for two tasks: tumor versus normal in the PZ ($n = 81$) and in the TZ ($n = 68$). Detection classifiers were trained separately by zone, because normal PZ and TZ tissue differ systematically in baseline diffusion whereas tumor tissue is comparatively similar across zones, so a pooled boundary would conflate zonal and pathological variation. We did not compute grade-classification AUCs: with only 29 graded tumors they are too imprecise to be informative (95\% intervals spanning $\sim$0.5--0.95); grading is instead analyzed as grade-dependent spectral shifts (Results).

For each task we compared four feature sets through the identical LOOCV pipeline---ADC alone; each individual spectral fraction alone; the two outer fractions $\{D = 0.25,\,3.0\;\umsmm\}$; and all eight fractions (MAP or NUTS posterior means)---fitting a standardized logistic-regression classifier ($C = 1.0$) within each fold, with standardization and fitting confined to the training fold. Passing single- and multi-feature models through the same held-out pipeline keeps them comparable. For reference we additionally report the conventional in-sample ADC raw-rank AUC (Table~\ref{tab:auc}), the most conservative baseline; the fair model-versus-model comparison is ADC logistic regression against the spectral classifiers. Performance was the area under the ROC curve (AUC). Ninety-five percent confidence intervals for all AUCs and for the ROI-level ADC--discriminant correlations were obtained by patient-level percentile bootstrap (2000 resamples) \citep{efron1993bootstrap}, the single interval convention used throughout (no separate AUC significance test is reported). Results were robust to the regularization strength (AUC varied by $< 0.03$ between $C = 0.1$ and $10$).

The ADC sensitivity vector (Eq.~\ref{eq:sensitivity}) was evaluated at the average tumor and average normal spectra per zone by finite differences ($\epsilon = 10^{-4}$), and its alignment with the eight-dimensional logistic-regression coefficient vector quantified by Pearson and Spearman correlation.

\subsection{Pixel-wise and Direction-wise Estimation}

To demonstrate the framework at the voxel level, pixel-wise decomposition was performed on a single axial slice from one patient \stephan{please confirm whether this patient is within the study cohort; this matters because the discriminant uses cohort-trained coefficients}, with the prostate manually segmented. Each voxel's $b = 0$-normalized decay was decomposed by MAP (Eq.~\ref{eq:map}) and NUTS (Eqs.~\ref{eq:prior_R}--\ref{eq:likelihood}), and a spectral discriminant score computed by applying the PZ classifier coefficients (the segmented lesion lay in the PZ) to the voxel spectra; for NUTS, discriminant uncertainty was propagated by passing 500 posterior draws through the classifier. Applying an ROI-trained classifier at the voxel level is a deliberate feasibility demonstration whose limitations are discussed in Discussion.
