% --- Fig 1: Spectra + Identifiability Combined ---
\begin{figure}[p]
\centering
% TODO(figure-regen): increase font size by ~50% throughout (axis labels,
% tick labels, legends, panel titles).
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig_spectra_combined.pdf}
\caption{%
Diffusivity spectra by tissue type with MAP and NUTS estimates.
Box plots show, for each diffusivity bin, the cohort distribution of the
estimated water fraction $R_j$: box edges at the 25th and 75th percentiles,
center line at the median, whiskers extending to 1.5$\times$ the
interquartile range, and outliers shown as individual points.
Top row: normal-tissue ROIs; bottom row: tumor ROIs.
Left column: peripheral zone (PZ); right column: transition zone (TZ).
Within each panel, MAP estimates (green) are plotted alongside NUTS
posterior-mean estimates (orange).
NUTS concentrates mass at the dominant component ($D = 3.0\;\umsmm$ in normal
tissue), whereas MAP (ridge regression) smooths across adjacent bins due to
the $L_2$ penalty.
Per-panel posterior coefficient of variation (CV $=$ posterior std / posterior
mean), averaged over each panel's ROIs, is annotated above each subplot.
Identifiability is tissue-dependent: in normal tissue the free-water peak
($D = 3.0\;\umsmm$) is well resolved (PZ CV $\approx 0.19$, TZ CV $\approx 0.30$),
whereas in tumor it loses identifiability (CV $\approx 0.55$--$0.59$); the
restricted-diffusion bin ($D = 0.25\;\umsmm$) shows the inverse pattern,
better identified in tumor (CV $\approx 0.12$--$0.18$) than normal
(CV $\approx 0.17$--$0.25$).
Intermediate bins ($D = 0.50$--$1.00\;\umsmm$) are uniformly poorly
identified across all panels (CV $> 0.7$).
}
\label{fig:spectra}
\end{figure}

ADC is a near-optimal spectral discriminant.
ADC versus the spectral discriminant score (logistic regression linear

\caption{%
ADC sensitivity aligns with the optimal classification direction.
Normalized ADC sensitivity vector ($-\partial\text{ADC}/\partial R_j$) and
logistic regression coefficients for each diffusivity component.
(Left)~PZ: $r = -0.979$ (all 8), $r = -0.982$ (excluding $D = 20\;\umsmm$).
(Right)~TZ: $r = -0.931$ (all 8), $r = -0.941$ (excluding $D = 20\;\umsmm$).
Sensitivity evaluated at the average tumor spectrum (MAP features,
$C = 1.0$).
The smaller correlation when $D = 20\;\umsmm$ is included reflects the fact
that ADC is less sensitive to the vascular compartment: although $b = 0$ is
included in the fit, the $D = 20\;\umsmm$ signal has decayed to near zero at
all other b-values, leaving only the intercept to constrain this component.
Excluding $b = 0$ has been reported to alter discrimination performance in
either direction depending on cohort and protocol \citep{aksitciris2019accelerated},
and is discussed further in the Discussion.
}
\label{fig:sensitivity}
\end{figure}

% --- Fig 8: Fisher Information Analysis ---
\begin{figure}[p]
\centering
% TODO(figure-regen): larger fonts (~+50%); in panel (b) move the legend to
% a free area in the middle of the plot so that it does not cover the bars
% or the improvement-factor labels.
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig_fisher.pdf}
\caption{%
Fisher information analysis of the spectral discretization.
(a)~Fisher information correlation matrix: adjacent diffusivity components
($D = 0.50$--$1.00\;\umsmm$) have mutual correlation $> 0.99$, making them
fundamentally indistinguishable from 15 b-value measurements.
$D = 0.25$ and $D = 20.0\;\umsmm$ are the most separable components.
(b)~Unconstrained Cram\'er-Rao lower bound (gray) versus observed NUTS
posterior standard deviation (orange) at SNR = 150.
Labels show the improvement factor.
Non-negativity constraints and Bayesian priors reduce estimation error by
100--8000$\times$, demonstrating their essential role.
(c)~Individual component decay curves $R_j \exp(-b D_j) / K$ at equal
fractions, with horizontal noise floors at SNR = 50--300.
Slow-diffusing components (e.g., $D = 0.25\;\umsmm$) contribute signal
across all b-values; fast components ($D \geq 3.0\;\umsmm$) decay below the
noise floor within the first few b-value steps, motivating the sparser
discretization at high diffusivities.
}
\label{fig:fisher}
\end{figure}
